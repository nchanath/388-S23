Final exam guidelines and suggestions

As promised, here’s some information that hopefully will help you prepare for the upcoming final exam in this course.

Facts about the final exam:

  • Length: 3 hours
  • Types of questions: a few true/false questions, a few essay questions, at most one reduction, a few attacks, some basic questions in which you’d have to compute some things
  • Level of difficulty: similar to homework. Make sure you know what a proof of insecurity and a proof of security should contain.

What you should focus on to prepare for the final exam:

  • my book
  • lecture slides
  • class notes that you took during lecture
  • homework
  • case study readings from Boneh-Shoup
  • big-picture concepts underlying the case studies.

Notably absent from the above are the Boneh-Shoup textbook reading assignments that are not for the case studies. These reading assignments were assigned to help you follow my lectures on the relevant topics. But, in order to prepare for the final exams on these topics, you should focus on my lecture slides. The reason for this is that some material in the Boneh-Shoup textbook, e.g., game-hopping proofs, is too advanced especially for a time-constrained exam.

Overall suggestions

Beyond the details mentioned above, the most important thing I’d like you to be able to do is, to borrow Phil’s words, to internalize the important security definitions, e.g., IND-CPA, IND-CCA, WUF-CMA, SUF-CMA, PRF, PRP-CPA, PRP-CCA. You should also be able to intuitively relate these definitions to their variations (e.g., like we did for IND-CPA and ROR-CPA). To demonstrate these abilities, it is important that you can express your ideas clearly, which as you have seen throughout the semester can be best achieved through writing clear pseudocode and probability analysis.

Cheat sheets

The exam is closed book, closed notes, but cheat sheets are encouraged. You are allowed a total of 4 pages on 2 letter-sized sheets. Please write on it in your own handwriting unless for some reason you truly cannot.
